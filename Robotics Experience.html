About Me

Robotics Experience

Robotics Team Captain, for the First Robotics Competition at Canoga Park High School

The engineering cycle is composed of 4 key components: Design, Build, Test, Improve. Throughout our 1st year as part of the HIP Program we had different competitions that would further develop our fundamentals of engineering. These robotics competitions that I had with my peers wouldn't have been possible without using the engineering cycle, because when we would think our robot is complete, there was still more we can further improve on. This allowed us to know that just like in a product for a company, this cycle must be further gone through in order to fully develop a solid product for a consumer.

Our challenges include of the following:

  • Drag Race
  • Pick-Up
  • Team-Robot
  • Point A-Point B
  • Basketball

These challenges helped us understand different fundamentals through different tasks. I ended up winning the Drag Race, Pick-Up, Team-Robot, and split the Basketball competition. They also taught us very important lessons, such as: don't procrastinate, ask for help, and keeping your design simple enough for you to understand.

Along with the competitions, the HIP Program also enhanced our knowledge, not only about fundamental conceps, but also for our careers. Through discussions, and after-lunch activities, mentors developed our knowledge of what a network is, how to further develop a resume, how to apply for financial aid, and the common app. Along with these activities, we were able to attend a tour at the Viterbi School of Engineering at the USC campus.
